    <header>Phishing Awareness: Stay Safe Online at College</header>
    <div class="container">
        <h2>What is Phishing?</h2>
        <p>Phishing is a cyberattack where hackers impersonate legitimate sources to steal sensitive information, such as passwords, financial details, and personal data. College students are prime targets due to frequent online interactions.</p>
        
        <h2>How to Spot a Phishing Email</h2>
        <ul>
            <li class="warning">"Urgent action required" emails about tuition, grades, or student loans</li>
            <li>Fake emails pretending to be from your university or professors</li>
            <li>Poor grammar and spelling mistakes</li>
            <li>Suspicious links or attachments</li>
            <li>Requests for login credentials or personal data</li>
        </ul>
        
        <h2>Common Phishing Scams for Students</h2>
        <ul>
            <li class="warning">Fake internship or job offers requesting personal details</li>
            <li>Scam scholarship emails asking for banking information</li>
            <li>Fake campus Wi-Fi login pages stealing passwords</li>
            <li>Emails claiming issues with student loans or tuition payments</li>
        </ul>
        
        <h2>What to Do If You Suspect Phishing?</h2>
        <p>Never click on suspicious links. Report phishing emails to your university IT department or email provider. Use multi-factor authentication to protect your accounts.</p>
